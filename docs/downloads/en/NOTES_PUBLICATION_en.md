## Abstract for Zenodo (~250 words)

**Suggested title**:
> A Structural Reading of Fundamental Physics: Phase 1 Extraction

**Abstract (English)**:

This repository presents the complete structural extraction of an alternative cosmological model intuitively carried since 2006 and formalized in extended sessions between April 24 and May 7, 2026. The model proposes that a single energetic entity (e) at a structural point (t=0), inscribed within a circular and constant temporal axis (T), and addressed via a generative function 4df(x), suffices to account for the full range of observed physical phenomena—particles, forces, masses, charges, dark energy, quantum mechanics. The model is not a correction to the Standard Model, but a self-contained reading from 9 fundamental postulates, 160 canonical pieces (Q1-Q160 except Q78-Q79), and 300 inferences tested on standard physics questions. It structurally predicts resolutions to several open problems (dark matter, force hierarchy, quantum measurement problem, information paradox). Phase 1 structural extraction is complete and coherent; Phase 2 (mathematical formalization of 4df(x) with IN cost and OUT consumption as fixed contributions) remains to be undertaken by a qualified collaborator. Falsification criteria are explicit: no individually detectable dark matter, no 4th lepton family, no fundamental SUSY. The corpus comprises 11 files totaling ~14,000 lines, with preservation of the author's raw verbatims as a method of structural reading. Includes a dedicated file on demonstrated physical observations (09_DEMONSTRATED_PHYSICAL_OBSERVATIONS) covering cosmology, particles, forces, quantum mechanics, condensed matter, astrophysics, classical mechanics, and precision technologies. The author's late-session refinements (May 6-7) include a unifying principle for all observed precessions (Mercury, Larmor, Dzhanibekov) and a first Phase 2 equation: the neutrino-photon comparative equation as a strategy for empirical calibration of 4df(x).

## Discreet publication strategy (per author's decision)

The author has explicitly chosen a **discreet publication** rather than active promotion. Justification: minimize initial feedback to preserve time available for other ongoing projects.

**Recommendations**:

1. **Zenodo deposit** only (permanent DOI, citable). No journal submission.
2. **Public Git repository** (GitHub or GitLab) accessible but not promoted.
3. **No social networks** at the time of publication.
4. **No press release**.
5. **No arXiv submission** (which would require endorsement and potentially attract attention).
6. **Author's personal page** may mention the work as a reference, without call to engagement.

**Expected effects**:
- The Zenodo DOI allows citation by anyone who finds the corpus useful
- Passive indexing by academic search engines
- Natural discovery by interested researchers without initial deluge
- The author retains control of their time

**If engagement desired later**: the author can always signal the work via targeted channels (specific researchers contacted directly) without major revision.
